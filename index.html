Popular Destinations

Timeless heritage, vibrant culture, and breathtaking landscapes.

Kerala

Athirappilly Waterfalls

Often called the “Niagara of India,” is the largest waterfall in Kerala. Located in Thrissur district, it cascades dramatically amidst lush green forests of the Western Ghats. The roaring monsoon flow and scenic trekking paths make it a favorite for nature and adventure lovers. Its breathtaking beauty has also made it a popular filming location.

Tamil Nadu

Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple

One of the largest functioning Hindu temples in the world, this magnificent shrine in Srirangam is dedicated to Lord Ranganatha. Renowned for its towering gopurams and intricate architecture, it stands as a timeless symbol of devotion and Dravidian grandeur. Its intricate carvings and vast courtyards reflect centuries of devotion and artistic excellence.

Karnataka

Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its magnificent ruins and ancient temples. Once the glorious capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, it showcases grand architecture and rich history. Its dramatic landscape of giant boulders, stone chariots, and sacred riverbanks creates a surreal atmosphere.

Rajasthan

The Pink City

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is famously known as the “Pink City” for its distinctive rose-colored buildings. It is home to magnificent forts and palaces like Amer Fort and City Palace, reflecting royal heritage. The city blends vibrant culture, bustling bazaars, and stunning architecture. Jaipur offers a rich experience of history, tradition, and regal charm.

Himachal Pradesh

Spiti Valley

A high-altitude cold desert known for rugged landscapes and remote monasteries. Ideal for adventure biking, trekking, and camping under clear Himalayan skies. Its dramatic terrain offers an unforgettable offbeat experience. It lies in the eastern part of Himachal Pradesh, near the Indo-Tibetan border.

Meghalaya

Elephant Falls

Elephant Falls is located near the capital city of Shillong. It is a beautiful three-tiered waterfall surrounded by lush greenery and rocky terrain. Easily accessible and popular among tourists, it is one of the most visited natural attractions in Meghalaya. The falls are especially stunning during the monsoon season when the water flow is strong and vibrant.
